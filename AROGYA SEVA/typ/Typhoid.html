Clinical Evaluation:

Symptom Assessment: The doctor will first evaluate symptoms such as high fever, headache, abdominal pain, and gastrointestinal disturbances (diarrhea or constipation).

Medical History: They may ask about recent travel history, exposure to contaminated food or water, and whether the patient has been vaccinated against typhoid.

Laboratory Tests:

Blood Culture: The most common and reliable test to diagnose typhoid, where a sample of the patient's blood is taken and cultured to detect the presence of Salmonella Typhi bacteria. Blood cultures are most effective in the first week of infection when the bacteria are present in the bloodstream.

Stool Culture: A stool sample is tested for the presence of Salmonella Typhi. This is particularly useful in identifying carriers of the disease who may not show symptoms but can spread the bacteria.

Typhidot Test:

A rapid diagnostic test that detects IgM and IgG antibodies against Salmonella Typhi. It provides quicker results than cultures but may not be as reliable, particularly in early or late stages of infection.

Complete Blood Count (CBC):

Helps assess overall health and detect the presence of infection, anemia, or other complications associated with typhoid.
